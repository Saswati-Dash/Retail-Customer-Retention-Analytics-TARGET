# Retail Customer Retention Analytics – Target

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior and retention patterns for Target, a leading retail company. With increasing competition in the retail industry, retaining loyal customers has become critical.

The objective is to transform raw customer, transaction, and loyalty data into actionable insights using Power BI, helping the business understand churn, improve retention, and enhance customer lifetime value.


## Objective

To develop an interactive Customer Retention Dashboard that:

* Identifies churned, loyal, and high-value customers
* Analyzes the impact of loyalty programs and promotions
* Evaluates store and channel performance
* Provides actionable strategies to improve customer retention


## Dataset Description

The project uses multiple datasets:

### 1. Customer Demographics

* Customer ID, Age, Gender, Region, Income Level
* Membership details and preferred shopping channel

### 2. Customer Transactions

* Transaction details including date, amount, product category
* Promotion applied (Yes/No)

### 3. Store Locations

* Store type, region, and operational details

### 4. Loyalty Program

* Loyalty tier (Basic, Plus, Premium, Elite)
* Points earned and redeemed

### 5. Churn Data

* Customer churn status and reasons (Inactivity, Competitor, Low Engagement)


## Tools & Technologies

* Power BI
* Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)
* DAX (Calculated Columns & Measures)
* Data Modeling

---

## Project Workflow

### Data Cleaning & Modeling

* Cleaned and transformed multiple datasets using Power Query
* Created relationships between datasets
* Generated calculated columns like Membership Duration


### Churn & Retention Analysis

* Calculated churn rate KPI
* Analyzed churn across region, income level, channel, and loyalty tiers
* Built funnel visualization for customer lifecycle


### Repeat Purchase Analysis

* Segmented customers into low, medium, and high purchase groups
* Analyzed purchase frequency across demographics and regions
* Identified top product categories among loyal customers

### Promotion & Loyalty Impact

* Compared purchase behavior with and without promotions
* Analyzed churn rates across loyalty tiers
* Evaluated points earned vs redeemed


### Store & Channel Performance

* Compared performance across store types
* Analyzed churn rate by store and channel
* Evaluated relationship between store characteristics and retention


### Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

* Calculated CLV using customer spending and membership duration
* Segmented customers into high-value and low-value groups
* Analyzed CLV trends across regions and loyalty tiers


## Key Insights

* High-value customers are strongly linked to higher loyalty tiers
* Promotions increase short-term purchases but may not ensure long-term retention
* Certain regions show higher churn due to lower engagement
* Online channels show different retention patterns compared to physical stores
* Customers with higher CLV tend to have consistent purchase behavior


## Recommendations

* Focus on retaining high-value customers through personalized offers
* Improve loyalty program engagement to reduce churn
* Optimize promotion strategies for long-term retention
* Strengthen underperforming regions and channels
* Encourage repeat purchases through targeted campaigns


## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data analytics and visualization can help businesses understand customer behavior, reduce churn, and improve retention strategies through data-driven decision-making.
